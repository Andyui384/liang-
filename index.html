About Me

A simple, student-focused introduction to who I am

My name is Liang Zenghao, a high school student with a strong interest in robotics. Right now, I serve as the captain of my school’s Robotics Club. I keep an approachable, collaborative mindset and love working alongside other students to build functional, creative robot projects together.

I’m always eager to learn new things related to mechanical design and technical problem-solving. I believe the best way to grow is through hands-on practice, communication with teammates, and constant trial and error when our robot builds run into challenges.

My Core Skills

Abilities I’ve developed through Robotics Club work

Design is my primary creative skill, which I use to plan the layout, structure and function of all our club robots. I spend time sketching out mechanical frameworks, choosing suitable parts, and adjusting designs to make our robots more stable and easy to assemble for every team member.

Alongside design, problem solving is a skill I use every day in club activities. When robots fail to move, sensors don’t respond, or our builds fall apart during testing, I break down the issue step by step, test different fixes, and work with teammates to find reliable solutions efficiently.

Robotics Experience

All my robotics learning and activities take place within my school’s Robotics Club

All of my robotics experience comes entirely from participation in my school’s Robotics Club. I started as a regular member, learning basic robot construction, simple design ideas, and how to troubleshoot minor technical problems alongside senior students in the club.

After gaining consistent hands-on experience, I was chosen to become the club captain. In this role, I organize group building sessions, guide new student members through basic robot design, and lead team testing to solve mechanical and functional problems as a group. Every project I work on is planned, built and tested exclusively within our school robotics club community.
